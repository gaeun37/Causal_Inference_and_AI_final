Slow Progress
In Settling Strike.
London, Feb. 26-On the eve of the
resumption of the conference between
Premier Asquith and _ other cabinet
ministers and the miners' representatives
which marks tile most critical
stage of the government's efforts to
avoid a coal strike throughout the
United Kingdom, the nation is kept
actually in the dark as to what has
been accomplished in the direction of
securing peace since the negotiations
opened, on Feb. 22. On that date the
miners' delegation informed the premier
that they were powerless to
agree to anything, or to assume any
responsibility, until authorization has
had been obtained from the Miners'
Federation, which will meet, tomorrow,
in London.
Unless the owners agree to concede
a minimum wage, on which the miners
of all sections are inflexible, a national
strike would seem inevitable,
but there is undoubtedly an optimistic
feeling abroad that the government
practically has succeeded in persuading
the owners to yield, for a few
months at least.
