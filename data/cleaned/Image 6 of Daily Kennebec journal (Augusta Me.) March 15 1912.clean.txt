Republican Gains. Democratic Losses,
(Portland Express and Advertiser.)
The Democratic losses and the Republican
gains had not been figured
by the headline writer who proclaimed
a Democratic triumph yesterday. In
the four cities in which there was a
contest, Biddeford, Belfast, Bangor and
Augusta, the total vote for the Republican
candidates for mayor was 4724
and the Democratic vote was 6,036.
Two years ago in the election of Governor.
these same cities gave a Republican
vote of 3899 and a Democratic
vote of 6,434. So that the Republicans
gained in the four cities, 825 votes and
the Democrats lost 388 votes.
The Republican gain was a gain of
21 per cent, and the Democratic loss
was a loss ol six per cent. Rearing in
mind all the while that the cities are
still Democratic, and that the great
Republican strength in Maine is in the
rural districts, it is interesting to observe
that a gain of 21 per cent on the
vote of two years ago if extended over
the whole State the total Republican
vote for Governor being 64,672, would
give a total Republican vote of 78,253;
and that a Democratic loss of six per
cent on the total vote for Governor
Plaisted of 73,425, would give a total
Democratic vote of 69,020. which, subtracted
from the possible Republican
\ote, shows a Republican majority of
9,233.
Xow let us consider the 14 cities
which have held elections this spring.
The total Republican vote in these 14
cities is 12,253 against the total Democratic
vote of 14,901. Two years ago
on the vote lor Governor, the Republican
candidate in these same cities
received 10,696 and the Democratic
candidate 16,568. This shows a Republican
gain in the 14 cities of 1,557
votes and a Democratic loss in the
same cities of 1,667 votes. The Republican
gain is a g;f!n of 14.6 per cent,
and the Democratic loss is a loss of
10.1 per cent. Applying tiie same per -
centage of gain and loss to the total

and a total Democratic vote of 66,009.
or a Republican majority of 8,105; and

showing a 'trend toward the Republican

by the Republican gain in the rural
sections of the State.
