The first contested divorce case to
be tried in the supreme court, this
term, was Saturday, that of Mrs. Ada
Norton vs. Arthur Norton of North
New Portland. A cross libel was filed
by the husband and both Mitels alleges
cruel and abusive treatment. The
story told by Mrs. Norton was pitiful.
She testified that they had been married
since 1900. She said he had struck
her with a club, twisted her nose,
pinched her, and that her mother-inlaw
had interfered continually and
made life miserable. Bernard Gibbs
of Madison appeared for Mrs. Norton
and argued that the husband's parents
were not capable of caring for the two
older children. Judge Augustine Simmons
appeared for the husband and
asked for the custody of the two older
children. Justice Bird reserved his decision
in the matter until later.
