MEN IDLE, CHILDREN AT WORK.
There will be held in the near future the national conference
convention on child labor, it being the eighth annual convention of
the organization. In the 'years in which the organization has beeb
in existence there has been a considerable reduction in the number
of children who arc employed in manufacturing, mining and other
occupations, due in part to prohibitory legislation and also by making
attendance on school compulsory. But there are yet tens of
thousands of children in this, country who are being worked to their
detriment, phj'sical, mental and moral
While the children arc "employed in gainful occupations, the
New York Tribune wants to know what of the men who are supposed
to be the workers and the breadwinners? A' great many men
are constantly idle, not alone through strikes and lockouts, but
through an overcrowding of tho labor market. There is thus presented
the strange spectacle of men unemployed while small children
and women do the work. Doubtless industries are far more
extonsively crowded with women than with children, and perhaps
no less perniciously. It is a common complaint of young men that
they cannot get employment because all tho places are filled with
women and girls, who work for lower wages than men can afford
to do, and an ominous racial effect is thus being produced in the
discouragement of marriage and the founding of families. But
that is a far more difficult matter to deal with than child labor. It
would be exceedingly radiqal legislation which should forbid women
pf age to engage in any honest occupation in which they had an
opportunity.
The propriety of closely restricting child labor, even regardless
of the wishes of the parents and of the children themselves, is generally
conceded, and the need of it is increasingly apparent. It will
not serve to say that the work must be done and therefore the children
must do it. It can be done by adults. Perhaps in that way it
will cost more, but that is not after all the chief consideration. It
would be monstrous to say that the children must be sacrificed for
the sake of cheap production, no matter whether that cheapness
meant more profits to the employers or lower cost to the consumer
So long as men are idle all over the land it is u reproach that we
are making slaves of minor children.
