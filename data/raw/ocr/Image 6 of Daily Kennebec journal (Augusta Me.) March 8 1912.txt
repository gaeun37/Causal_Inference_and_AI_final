Asiatics Are . Losing Ground in
Australian Pearl Fisheries.
Tin* Australian Federal Government ha <
decreed Unit Asiatic divers employed in
the Australian I Vai l Fisheries are to be
replaced by whites, and a number of exnavnl
divers from Holland are to be sent
out for tin- work to Broome, West Australia.
Of late years pearl fishing has
h» en carried on with considerable success
in tin' Australian seas. Good pearlare
found in Shark’s Bay, on the coast of
West Australia, especially in an inlet
termed Fseless Harbor. Mother-of-pearl
shells are also fished at many other
points along1 the western coast, between
the J5th and 25th parallels of south latitude.
An important pearl fishery is also
established in Torres Straits, and on the
coast of Queensland.
The shells occur in water from four to
six fathoms deep, and the divers are genii
ally Malays and Papuans, though
sometimes native Australians. On the
western coast of Australia the pearl
shells are frequently obtained by dredging.
The old style of pearl diving byAsiatics
is done by the diver descending
without any diver’s apparatus, and remaining
under water as long as possible.
Some of these men have a marvellous endurance,
the usual time under water being
from 50 to So seconds, although it has
been stated that cases have been known
for a man to remain under water for as
long as five minutes. Judging from the
large amount of Asiatic divers at the
present time engaged in the pearl fisheries
around the Australian coasts, it
would seem that the Federal Government
has undertaken a rather large order
in proposing to fill their places with
white divers.—-Shipping Illustrated.
